Court Construction FAQs

Answers to common questions about our court construction services

How long does it take to construct a sports court?

The timeline varies based on court type, size, and site conditions. Generally, a standard tennis court takes 4-6 weeks from excavation to completion. Basketball courts typically take 2-4 weeks, while multi-sport facilities may require 6-8 weeks. Complex projects or those requiring extensive site preparation may take longer.

What's the best surface for a multi-purpose court?

For multi-purpose courts, modular sports tiles or acrylic surfaces are often recommended. Modular tiles offer excellent shock absorption and can be used for various sports. Acrylic surfaces provide good durability and can be lined for multiple sports. The best choice depends on your primary use, budget, and maintenance preferences.

How much maintenance do sports courts require?

Maintenance requirements vary by surface type. Acrylic courts need occasional cleaning and resurfacing every 4-8 years. Modular tiles require minimal maintenance beyond occasional cleaning. Artificial grass needs brushing and occasional infill top-ups. We provide detailed maintenance guidelines for each court type we install.

Can courts be installed on sloped surfaces?

Yes, we can construct courts on sloped terrain through proper grading and retaining wall systems. However, significant slope correction adds to the project cost and timeline. We assess each site individually and provide solutions that work with the natural topography while ensuring proper drainage and playability.
