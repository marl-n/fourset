Paving Materials We Work With

We specialize in various paving materials to suit different styles and budgets

Brick Pavers

Classic clay brick pavers offer timeless appeal with excellent durability. Available in various colors, sizes, and patterns to complement any architectural style.

Cobblestone Pavers

Natural stone cobblestones provide a historic, rustic charm with exceptional longevity. Ideal for creating old-world elegance in modern settings.

Bevel Pavers

Concrete bevel pavers with slightly angled edges create visual interest and help with drainage. Available in numerous colors and textures.

Interlocking Pavers

Interlocking concrete pavers create strong, stable surfaces that can withstand heavy use. Perfect for driveways and high-traffic areas.

Natural Stone Pavers

Natural stone including slate, bluestone, travertine, and limestone offers unique beauty with each piece being one-of-a-kind.

Permeable Pavers

Eco-friendly permeable pavers allow water to seep through, reducing runoff and supporting sustainable drainage solutions.

Paving Services FAQs

Answers to common questions about our paving services

How long does a paved surface typically last?

With proper installation and maintenance, paved surfaces can last 20-25 years or more. Brick and stone pavers often have the longest lifespan, while concrete pavers typically last 15-20 years. Regular maintenance like joint sand replenishment and occasional sealing can extend the life of your paved surfaces.

What's the difference between brick pavers and concrete pavers?

Brick pavers are made from clay and fired in a kiln, offering a classic look and natural color variations. Concrete pavers are made from a mixture of cement and aggregates, offering more uniform appearance and a wider range of color options. Brick tends to be more expensive but offers timeless appeal, while concrete provides excellent durability and cost-effectiveness.

Can paving be installed in any weather?

Ideal paving conditions are dry with moderate temperatures. We avoid paving during heavy rain or extreme heat, as these conditions can affect the installation quality. In winter, paving is generally not recommended when temperatures are near or below freezing. We monitor weather forecasts carefully and schedule projects accordingly to ensure optimal results.

How much maintenance do paved surfaces require?

Paved surfaces require relatively low maintenance. Regular sweeping and occasional washing are usually sufficient. We recommend reapplying joint sand every 2-3 years and sealing every 3-5 years for maximum protection and appearance. For areas with weed growth, occasional weed control may be necessary.

Can paving be installed over an existing concrete surface?

Yes, in many cases pavers can be installed over existing concrete surfaces, provided the concrete is in good condition with no significant cracks or settling. This can be a cost-effective option as it eliminates the need for demolition and base preparation. We assess each situation individually to determine if overlay installation is appropriate.
