Got Questions? We've Got Answers!

Answers to common questions about our asphalt and tar services

How long does a new asphalt driveway typically last?

With proper installation and maintenance, a new asphalt driveway can last 15-20 years. Regular sealcoating every 2-3 years can extend its life significantly. Factors like climate, usage, and base preparation also affect longevity.

What's the best time of year for asphalt installation?

Asphalt is best installed in warmer months (spring through early fall) when temperatures are consistently above 10°C. This allows for proper compaction and curing. However, we have techniques to work in cooler conditions when necessary.

How long until I can drive on new asphalt?

For light vehicle traffic, you should wait at least 24-48 hours. For heavier traffic or parking, we recommend waiting 3-5 days. Full curing takes about 6-12 months, but the surface is functional within days of installation.

Can asphalt be installed over an existing concrete surface?

Yes, asphalt can be installed over concrete if the concrete is in good condition with no significant cracks or settling. We first assess the existing surface and may recommend repairs or a full removal depending on the condition.

How often should asphalt be sealcoated?

We recommend sealcoating every 2-3 years for optimal protection. High-traffic areas may need more frequent applications. The first sealcoat should be applied 6-12 months after new asphalt installation.
